# Jenga365 Logical Framework: Sport for Change

## 1. Overview: The Theory of Change
We utilize the rugby community and its inherent discipline as a primary resource to champion environmental and social sustainability, collaborating with like-minded organizations to facilitate systemic change.

## 2. Framework Hierarchy

| Level | Components |
|---|---|
| **Impact (Goal)** | **Environmental and Social Sustainability** |
| **Outcomes (Mid-term)** | Green Spaces, Cleaner Estates, Improved Welfare, Quality Education, High Employment Rate, Increased Morality |
| **Outputs (Immediate)** | • 1,000+ Trees planted/growing<br>• 20% Reduction in plastic waste<br>• Cleaner drainages<br>• 20% Reduction in Gender-Based Violence (GBV)<br>• 30% Increased school attendance<br>• 100+ Interviews attended<br>• 10+ Mentees employed |
| **Activities** | • Tree planting & Cleanups<br>• Civic Education on ESG<br>• Webinars & Network Clinics<br>• Mentorship sessions<br>• Site visits & Volunteering |
| **Inputs** | • **Community**: Rugby community, Public community<br>• **Partners**: NGOs, CBOs, Corporate Partners<br>• **Resources**: Time, Money, Media coverage |

## 3. Core Strategy: [SPORTS FOR CHANGE / IMPACT]
Equipping members and participants with the agency to act as ambassadors for sustainability across their local environments.
